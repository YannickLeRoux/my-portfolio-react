After working for ten years as a sound engineer and DJ in the music industry, I felt a desire to switch career early 2017.
I remember that as a kid and then a teenager, I loved to create small scripts on my Atari STE, or even hack into some games. I just didn't know at that time if I had what it would take to, one day, become a professional developer.

Then I discovered the impressive range and quality of online content designed to teach people, like me, how to write code.
I asked some informed people for advice and was told to give Python a try.

I quickly completed a first course created by MIT, Introduction to Computer Science and Python, and achieved a passing grade.
I enjoyed the course so much I became hooked on learning more about how to code.

Time to get my first developer job!

The first personal projects I undertook were driven by the idea of making my current job easier. Organizing the music files in my laptop or reading through tons of blog articles and charts to collect good tracks to play, could be very boring and repetitive. These things, I felt, could definitely be helped by coding.

For several years, I had been building websites using WordPress, but once I knew how to code, I wanted to know how to build a website from scratch.

Well, it so happens that Python is good at that too! As I was already familiar with HTML and CSS, I got into Django to learn to create the back-end.
My first project was to create a receipts organizer app, one that could scan receipts and keep them organized in a database. I still have to make some improvements to it (i.e. the facility to scan with a phone, design, security, etc.,) but anybody can sign up for an account and import documents already scanned.

I next decided to learn JavaScript, following the University of Pennsylvania online course, as I believe it is essential for Web Development.

Django is amazing but it's sometimes too much boilerplate for very small apps like this one, so I learned how to use Flask as well.

These days, I continue to dive deeper into programming, extending my front-end knowledge learning React JS (web app currently in development), and deployment tools like Docker. I am also improving my ability to deploy my apps on a Linux server as I firmly believe Web apps belong to the Web !
